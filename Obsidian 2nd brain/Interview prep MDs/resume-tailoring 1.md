# resume-tailoring.md

Primary playbook for tailoring Marc Agosin's resume to a specific job description. Load this for every tailoring request.

---

## PART 1 — JD ANALYSIS PROTOCOL

Before touching the resume, decompose the JD into these components. Do this work internally; surface a condensed version to Marc.

### 1.1 Extract hard requirements
Things the JD explicitly requires. Look for: "required," "must have," "X+ years of," "experience with," and the first 3–5 bullets of the responsibilities/qualifications sections (usually the load-bearing ones).

### 1.2 Extract preferred qualifications
"Nice to have," "bonus," "preferred," "plus." These are tiebreakers, not gatekeepers. Include if Marc has them; don't force if he doesn't.

### 1.3 Build the ATS keyword inventory
Capture:
- **Hard skills / tools** (e.g., SQL, Jira, Figma, Amplitude, specific frameworks)
- **Methodologies** (e.g., PLG, discovery, A/B testing, roadmapping, OKRs)
- **Domain terms** (e.g., embedded payments, B2B SaaS, fintech, AdTech)
- **Role-specific phrases** (e.g., "0 to 1," "product-led growth," "cross-functional leadership")
- **Seniority signals** (e.g., "lead," "principal," "staff," "senior," "director")

Note exact phrasing. ATS matches strings — "product-led growth" and "PLG" should BOTH appear if both show up in the JD.

### 1.4 Infer the "job to be done"
What is the hiring manager actually trying to solve by making this hire? Read between the lines:
- Growth-stage company with "scale our X" language → they need someone who's done it before at size
- Repeated mentions of "ambiguity" or "0 to 1" → they need a starter, not an optimizer
- Heavy on "stakeholder management" → internal complexity is the real problem
- AI/ML-forward JD → they want someone who ships AI features, not just someone who uses AI tools

Write this as one sentence. It's the compass for every bullet choice.

### 1.5 Identify scope and seniority signals
- Team size they'll manage or influence
- Revenue/user scope
- Reporting level
- Whether this is an IC track or people-manager track

### 1.6 Output of JD analysis to Marc
Give Marc a tight summary:
- **Role in one line:** "Senior PM, growth/PLG, B2B SaaS, IC track, reporting to Head of Product"
- **Hiring manager's real problem (1 sentence)**
- **Top 5–8 keywords to hit**
- **Hard-requirement gaps, if any** (be honest)

Keep this under ~15 lines. It's orientation, not a report.

## PART 2 — GAP ANALYSIS

Map Marc's master resume PLUS `marc-facts.md` (the durable fact corpus) against the JD on three buckets. The facts file is a legitimate source of experience and should be treated as such during gap analysis, alongside the master resume.

### 2.1 Strong matches
Experience where Marc's bullets or facts in `marc-facts.md` already prove the JD's requirement. Action: amplify with JD-native language, move earlier in the bullet order if needed. If the supporting detail comes from the facts file rather than the master resume, verify with Marc before using it as a load-bearing claim in a bullet (see Part 3.7).

### 2.2 Partial matches
Experience that is adjacent or underframed. Action: rewrite the bullet using JD language while staying factually accurate to what Marc did. This is where most of the tailoring work happens. The facts file often fills the detail gap here (team size, specific methods, artifact names) without requiring a new interview.

### 2.3 Gaps
Requirements Marc doesn't meet. Action options:
- **Omit** if it's a preferred qual and the resume is already strong
- **Flag to Marc** as a cover-letter opportunity
- **Reframe** if there's genuine adjacent experience (only if honest)
- **Acknowledge** in your tailoring notes — do not paper over

If the Explicit Gaps section of `marc-facts.md` contains a matching entry, treat the gap as already confirmed. Do not try to reframe it. Go straight to flag/omit.

Never invent to close a gap.

## PART 3 — BULLET REWRITING RULES

### 3.1 Bullet anatomy
Every bullet should answer three questions:
1. **What did Marc do?** (strong verb, specific action)
2. **How / with what leverage?** (tools, methods, scope, cross-functional levers)
3. **So what?** (outcome, metric, business impact)

Format: `[Verb] + [specific action with scope/method] + [quantified or concrete outcome]`

### 3.2 Verb selection
Lead with verbs that match the JD's seniority and the actual work. Prefer:
- Drove, led, owned, shipped, launched, scaled, architected, redesigned, unlocked, accelerated, overturned, prioritized, partnered, instrumented, synthesized, validated, reframed

Avoid:
- Helped, assisted, supported, contributed to, was responsible for, worked on, participated in, involved in

Downgrade verbs when the seniority doesn't justify them. "Spearheaded a category-defining transformation" on a small project reads as inflated.

### 3.3 Metrics
Every bullet should have a number OR a concrete artifact/scope. Categories:
- **Business outcomes:** ARR, revenue, % plan, conversion, retention, CAC, LTV
- **Product outcomes:** adoption, activation, NPS, time-to-value, release velocity, cycle time
- **Scope markers:** team size, # of customers, # of SKUs/products, $ in transactions, hours of research
- **Efficiency:** % improvement, time saved, cost reduced

If a bullet has no number, it must have a concrete noun: a named product, a named framework, a named customer segment, a specific artifact shipped.

### 3.4 Keyword integration
Integrate ATS keywords naturally into bullets, not in a list at the top. Rules:
- Use the JD's exact phrasing when Marc's actual experience matches
- Keep both acronym and expansion when both appear in the JD (e.g., "PLG (product-led growth)" the first time)
- Don't repeat a keyword more than twice across the resume unless it's a core theme
- Never force a keyword where it distorts the truth

### 3.5 Length per bullet
- 1–2 lines in final rendered form
- Cut adjectives ("innovative," "robust," "scalable") unless they add specific meaning
- Cut prepositions where possible
- If a bullet needs three lines, it's probably two bullets

### 3.6 Bullet ordering within a role
Order by relevance to the target JD, not by chronology or by what Marc is proudest of. The first bullet of each role should hit the JD's biggest requirement.

### 3.7 Verify-before-use for file-sourced facts
When a bullet pulls its key number, scope, or outcome from `marc-facts.md` (not from the master resume), briefly verify with Marc before finalizing if the fact is unusually load-bearing or the JD context is unusual. Example:

> "Using the team of 8 engineers and 1 designer from the facts file as the scope marker here — still accurate for this JD?"

Rules:
- Verify load-bearing facts when context is unusual. Do not verify every supporting detail every time — that defeats the purpose of the file.
- If Marc confirmed a fact within the last few sessions and it's still in the file, use it without re-verifying unless the JD specifically requires more precision.
- If the facts file and the master resume conflict, default to the master resume unless Marc has explicitly updated the facts file more recently.
- Never combine two file-sourced facts into a causal claim Marc did not make. Example: if the file has "team size 8" AND "25% plan beat" as separate entries, do not write "8-person team drove 25% growth" unless Marc's original framing supported that link.

If Marc is in a hurry and says "just use what you have," note the unverified sourcing in the tailoring notes.

## PART 4 — SECTION-SPECIFIC GUIDANCE

### 4.1 Summary Statement
- 2–3 sentences, max ~50 words
- Sentence 1: who Marc is (PM seniority + domain + a distinguishing signal)
- Sentence 2: what he does best, framed to the JD
- Sentence 3 (optional): a differentiator (AI-native, specific domain depth, etc.)
- Must include 3–5 of the top JD keywords, naturally

### 4.2 Professional Experience
- Keep Marc's three roles (ADP, EY-Parthenon, Accenture)
- Keep dates, titles, employers exact
- Bullet count guidance:
  - ADP (current, most relevant): 3–4 bullets
  - EY-Parthenon (recent, often highly relevant for strategy/discovery JDs): 3 bullets + selected engagements if space
  - Accenture (older, less relevant to PM roles): 1–2 bullets max, tightened
- Rotate which "selected engagements" appear under EY-Parthenon based on JD relevance. Marc's master has AdTech and GovTech examples — pick the one(s) that match, or swap in others if he has them

### 4.3 Education
- Keep as-is unless JD explicitly weights education differently
- Don't add GPA, coursework, or honors unless Marc requests

### 4.4 Skills, Certifications & Interests
- **Business Skills line:** reorder and trim to lead with JD-relevant skills
- **Tech Skills line:** reorder to lead with JD-relevant tools; drop items not relevant to this JD if space is tight
- **Certifications:** keep all, reorder by relevance
- **Languages:** keep
- **Interests:** keep but consider trimming if space-constrained; interests can humanize and sometimes spark rapport in interviews, so don't cut casually

## PART 5 — ATS OPTIMIZATION CHECKLIST

Before finalizing, verify:

- [ ] Standard section headers (Summary, Experience, Education, Skills) — no clever names
- [ ] No tables, text boxes, columns, headers/footers, or images in the output (Marc's is a .docx, so if he's pasting back in, flag this)
- [ ] Standard bullet character (• or -), not exotic glyphs
- [ ] Dates in consistent format (e.g., "March 2025 – Present")
- [ ] Job titles are industry-standard (not internal-only titles)
- [ ] Core keywords appear at least once; highest-priority keywords appear 2x if honest
- [ ] No keyword stuffing (a human reader must find it natural)
- [ ] File name suggestion: `Marc_Agosin_Resume_[Company]_[Role].docx`

## PART 6 — OUTPUT FORMAT

Deliver in this exact order:

### Section A — JD Read (condensed)
- Role in one line
- Hiring manager's real problem (1 sentence)
- Top keywords (comma-separated list)
- Any hard-requirement gaps

### Section B — Tailored Resume (Markdown)
Full resume, ready to paste. Use Marc's existing structure. Render in clean Markdown so he can transfer to the .docx.

### Section C — Tailoring Notes
Bullet list of the key changes you made and why. Keep this to 6–10 bullets. Marc wants to see the logic, not an essay.

### Section D — ATS Keyword Coverage
Two-column view:
- JD keyword → where it now appears in the resume (section/bullet)
- Missed keywords → reason (honest gap vs. redundant vs. unnatural)

### Section E — Flags & Next Moves
- Gaps to address in cover letter
- Bullets that would benefit from the interviewer module
- Suggested edits Marc might push back on (so he can overrule)

### Section F — Fact Capture
Per `fact-capture.md`. Emit a "FACT CAPTURE — SESSION END" block listing:
- Proposed additions to `marc-facts.md`, grouped by target section (ROLE 1 — ADP, ROLE 2 — EY-Parthenon, etc.)
- Any proposed replacements (OLD → NEW with reason)
- Facts considered but not proposed (and why)
- Notes for Marc (consolidation flags, section imbalances, etc.)

If this was a pure tailoring session with no new facts surfaced, say so: "No new facts this session." Do not fabricate capture entries to appear productive.

If the block has 5+ additions across 3+ sections, offer to regenerate the full `marc-facts.md` with additions integrated so Marc can download and replace rather than hand-edit.

## PART 7 — EDGE CASES

### 7.1 The JD is for a role Marc is clearly underqualified for
Say so up front. Explain the gap. Offer to tailor anyway if he wants to take the shot, but don't pretend the fit is there.

### 7.2 The JD is for a role that's a lateral or step-down
Tailor without inflating. Lead with depth and breadth signals rather than seniority signals.

### 7.3 The JD is AI/ML-product-focused
Lead with Marc's AI-native PM framing from ADP, RAG pipelines, prompt engineering, and Claude Code / AI agents from his skills. Be careful to distinguish "AI-native PM tooling" from "shipping AI products to customers" — these are different things to a hiring manager. If the JD wants the latter, flag honestly.

### 7.4 The JD heavily weights a domain Marc hasn't worked in (e.g., healthcare, gaming)
Flag the domain gap in the JD read. Emphasize transferable signals (regulated industry experience from ADP/consulting, pattern recognition from 400+ hours of executive interviews, etc.).

### 7.5 The JD is from a company Marc might be ambivalent about
Not your call. Tailor as requested. If he asks for your read on the role, you can give one.

---

## PART 8 — WHAT NOT TO DO

- Do not produce a version of the resume with fabricated titles, metrics, or employers
- Do not use em-dashes in the final resume output
- Do not use emoji in the resume
- Do not add a "Professional Summary" paragraph longer than 3 sentences
- Do not add a "Core Competencies" grid/table
- Do not add photos, links beyond the ones Marc already has, or graphic elements
- Do not reorganize Marc's career history non-chronologically
- Do not pad with generic PM phrases ("passionate about building great products," "customer-obsessed leader") — these are dead words to a recruiter
